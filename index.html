Section 4

The full growth funnel

Acquisition → Activation → Engagement → Retention → Monetization. Individual campaigns do not fix structural leaks. This is the system view.

The north star metric
★
Revenue is the north star — always. The question is not "which metric" but "how do we decompose revenue so we can manage it?" The answer is the equation:

Revenue = MAU × ARPU

MAU tells you how many users are generating revenue this month. ARPU tells you how much each one is worth. If MAU grows and ARPU grows, revenue grows — and you understand why. If revenue grows but ARPU is falling, you are adding low-value users and the growth is fragile. If ARPU grows but MAU is flat, you are squeezing the existing base without expanding it.

MAU definition: users who completed at least one revenue-generating action in the last 30 days. A user who opens the app and checks their balance is not MAU. A yield accrual, card transaction, mining payout, trade executed, or loan drawn — those qualify. In every case EMCD earned something too.

Why MAU over WAU? Crypto platforms have inherent weekly volatility — BTC moves 10% in a day, users flood in, WAU spikes, the following week is quiet and it drops. You spend half your time explaining noise. MAU smooths that volatility and gives you a stable baseline for strategic decisions. Bitso tracked MAU. Revolut reports MAU. Nubank tracks MAU. WAU lives inside the growth team as a tactical instrument for campaign evaluation and A/B tests — not as an executive metric.
Why MAU and not revenue — the deliberate choice
QuestionAnswer
Why Revenue = MAU × ARPU?Bitso reported revenue as their north star — but what they were managing internally was active users and revenue per user. Reporting the output is clean for investors. Managing the inputs is how you actually grow. MAU × ARPU gives you both: the size of the active base and the value density. If revenue misses, you immediately know whether it is a MAU problem (not enough active users) or an ARPU problem (users not using enough products).
Why can't EMCD just track revenue today?Because with a 22.3% activation rate, unmeasured retention, a 16% attribution gap, and undefined ARPU by BU, revenue is a black box. You cannot optimize a number you cannot decompose. Before you can grow revenue intelligently you need to know: how many users are active monthly (MAU), how much each generates (ARPU), and which BUs and ICPs produce the highest value users (ARPPU by BU). With no retention baseline today, WAU would create false urgency and false confidence alternately — MAU gives enough data per period to distinguish real trends from market-driven spikes.
ARPU vs ARPPU — both requiredARPU (revenue / total MAU) is the board metric — it tells the CEO whether the average user is getting more valuable. ARPPU (revenue / paying users only) is the growth team metric — it surfaces where value is actually concentrated. In crypto, top 10% of users generate 70-80% of revenue. ARPU hides this. ARPPU by BU reveals it: if INDESQ ARPPU is 5× Coinhold ARPPU, every active trader you upgrade from Coinhold to INDESQ is worth 5× the ARPU investment.
The Revolut parallelRevolut, Nubank, and Bitso all tracked MAU and revenue per user separately — the strategic conversation was always which lever to pull. When MAU growth slowed, they pushed ARPU (Metal card, insurance, trading, premium tiers). When ARPU was healthy, they invested in MAU. Every new product Revolut launched was fundamentally an ARPU play dressed as a product play. EMCD's Mining → Coinhold bridge, Coinhold → INDESQ upgrade, and card + Onlock cross-sell are all ARPU plays. Bitso used this exact framework — MAU as the user base metric, ARPU to manage revenue density.
